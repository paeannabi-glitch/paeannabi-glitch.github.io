About

I am an anthropologist, writer, photographer, and communications professional currently pursuing an MA in Medical Anthropology and Mental Health at SOAS University of London. Before moving to London, I completed my BSS in Anthropology at the University of Dhaka. My academic interests have grown alongside a lifelong involvement in writing, volunteering, visual storytelling, and public engagement.

I started writing while I was still in school. Over the years, that journey has taken me to platforms such as Prothom Alo, The Daily Star, Earki, and several other publications. Professionally, I have worked with organizations including BRAC, the Centre for Policy Dialogue (CPD), and the South Asian Network on Economic Modeling (SANEM). At CPD, I worked with the team behind the White Paper on the State of the Bangladesh Economy. I have also worked in advertising and communications, contributing to campaigns and content for major brands as well as development organizations.

Outside work and academia, I have always been drawn to community work and public causes. From volunteering in school clubs to participating in movements and protests that shaped public conversations in Bangladesh and beyond, civic engagement has remained a constant part of my life. These experiences continue to inform both my academic interests and my understanding of the world around me.

Many of these interests have also shaped my research. During my undergraduate studies, I worked on guestroom culture as my thesis topic at a time when discussions around student politics remained particularly sensitive. At BRAC, I worked on communication initiatives related to climate change, while my current interests focus on mental health, gender, digital culture, and the ways people experience power, belonging, and social change in everyday life.

When I am not working or studying, I am usually taking photographs, editing videos, writing, reading, or finding new ways to tell stories. Much of what I do, whether through research, writing, photography, or communication, begins with a curiosity about people, their lives, and the worlds they create around themselves.
